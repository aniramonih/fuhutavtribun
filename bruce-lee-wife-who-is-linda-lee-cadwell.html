Bruce Lee biography

The fifth and final child of Lee Hoi Chuen and Caucasian Chinese Grace Ho Oi-yee, Bruce Lee was born Lee Jun Fan on November 27, 1940 in San Francisco, California. Before Lee was born, his family relocated to the US; however, after Lee was three months old, they moved back to Hong Kong. A birth sister where he was born gave him the name Bruce. But until his later years, he was unable to use the name.

Before attending the University of Washington, where he studied in philosophy, Lee attended Edison Elementary School for his elementary school years in Washington. He studied judo, gymnastics, dance, poetry, and dance during his time at school. Lee’s studies incorporated a variety of Asian religions and beliefs since he had a particular interest in both Asian and Western philosophy. This helped him develop his love of poetry and his capacity for self-expression.

Lee had studied dance and trained in the Wing Chun style of kung fu under Yip Man before settling down to attend college in America. A mix of boxing and cha-cha dancing that he later evolved into a distinctive fighting style that finally became one of his legacies in Hong Kong.

Being an accomplished martial artist without being a master, Lee established a network of kung fu training facilities in Seattle where he imparted his unique style of fighting. Bruce Lee created history at his school when he became the first Caucasian in America to teach Kung Fu.

Lee accepted students without regard to their race or ethnicity, which ultimately brought him into conflict with Wong Jack Man when the legendary altercation between Wong Jack Man and Bruce Lee broke out.

Bruce Lee Wife: Who is Linda Lee Cadwell?

Linda Emery Lee Cadwell is an American author, martial artist, and retired instructor. She is the creator, a former trustee of, and an unpaid advisor of the Bruce Lee Foundation in addition to being the author of the biography of Bruce Lee titled Bruce Lee: The Man Only I Knew, which served as the basis for the movie Dragon: The Bruce Lee Story.

She first encountered Bruce Lee while studying martial arts, a discipline in which even the most hopeless circumstances can offer a sneaky way out. She has since endured both the devastating death of their son in 1993 and the untimely loss of her husband in 1973.

But despite the tragedy, she keeps growing and moving forward like a true martial arts student.

Her best-selling book Bruce Lee: The Man Only I Knew, which was later made into the biopic Dragon: The Bruce Lee Story, is only one of the many books she has written. Fans of Linda Lee Cadwell’s late husband value the work she created after using her personal tragedy as inspiration.
