His American nationality and African-American ethnicity are part of his unique identity that he often reflects in his work.

Stand-Up Comedy and SNL

Edwards, known for his clean comedy style and impressive voice acting skills, first gained major attention as a cast member of Saturday Night Live in 2001.

His talent for celebrity impersonations, including Michael Jackson, Chris Tucker, and Denzel Washington, quickly made him a fan favorite. Despite his brief tenure on SNL, Edwards left a lasting impact with his unique comedic style.

Voice Acting and Television Appearances

Edwards’ voice acting career is as diverse as his comedy. He voiced characters in Celebrity Deathmatch and Robotomy, and notably replaced Eddie Murphy as the voice of Donkey in Scared Shrekless.

Beyond voice roles, Edwards has appeared in television shows like The Sopranos and hosted the TV show Vidiots on TVone, expanding his presence in the entertainment industry.
