Wiki/Biography

Diipa Büller Khosla was born in Delhi, India, in August 1991. As of 2021, she will be 30 years old. Her family moved to Chennai when she was six years old, and then they moved to Ooty, Tamil Nadu. At first, she went to a Hindi-speaking school in Chennai. After that, she went to Hebron School in Ooty, Tamil Nadu.

Later, she got a scholarship to go to college in the Netherlands, which is in Europe. In 2012, she got her Bachelor of Laws in International Human Rights from the University College Utrecht in Utrecht, Netherlands. She got an LL.M. in Corporate Law from BPP Law School in London, UK, in 2015. In April 2013, she started working at the International Criminal Court in the Netherlands as a legal intern. Later, she did an internship at the Amsterdam office of the IMA Influencer Agency.

Husband and Kids

She met Oleg Büller for the first time when she was going to college in Amsterdam. Oleg works for the Dutch Foreign Service as a diplomat. She talked about the event in an interview. She told me,

I got there on the first day, but I was late because I have an Indian passport and had trouble getting a visa. So, most of the new students had already made friends and done everything else. My parents dropped me off at the main reception, and the first guy I saw is now my husband.”

Later, they met on Facebook, and their friendship quickly turned into love. After going out together for a few months, Oleg asked Diipa to marry him. In an interview, Diipa talked about how Oleg asked her to marry him. She told me,

He planned an event at the Devigarh Palace in Udaipur with the theme “Vogue India Christmas Dinner.” But when the day came, I found Oleg sitting on his knee at the end of this Indian palace’s beautiful courtyard. I was glad to not go to dinner!”

In September 2018, the couple got married at the Fateh Garh Palace in Udaipur, Rajasthan. The wedding took four days. They had wedding ceremonies in both the Punjabi and European styles. Sabyasachi Mukherjee and Piny Michaeli, two of India’s best-known fashion designers, made Diipa’s nine wedding outfits.

Career

In February 2014, she began working at IMA in Amsterdam, Netherlands, as a social media manager. In January 2015, she started working as a fashion influencer and put up her website, DiipaKhosla.com. In an interview, she talked about how she got to where she is now as a fashion influencer.

Wasn’t simple. It was only possible by working hard and setting very clear goals. I want to tell all the young people this. Know what you want to achieve. Think as clearly as if you were saying, “I want to be in Times Square!” You can figure out how and when it will happen, but just be clear.”

In 2019, she started the non-profit organization “Post For Change” to support women’s rights. She talked about her NGO in an interview. She told me,

Now, I’m working on a project to help get menstrual hygiene products to 250,000 rural women. That’s what I like best about what I do: I can have an effect on the things that have meant the most to me. I’ve been on the cover of international magazines, won awards for being an influencer, spoken to British Lords on Women’s Day, and been on billboards. But what really matters is that this platform has given me a reason to do something. It’s helped me turn all the bad things that have happened to me in my life into something good. It has given me a shield that lets me fight for those who can’t.”

I wanted to help people, so I went to school for Human Rights Law. I got my degree and then did an internship with an influencer agency in Europe. That’s where I found the world of social media. At first, I started a fashion blog. I was nervous, but I forced myself to leave my comfort zone. Over the next few years, my account grew, and I realized I had a voice that could reach thousands of people. So, I also made a page for women’s empowerment, which is a cause close to my heart. I wanted to help women who were having trouble with gender inequality and mental health issues.

She has also been on the covers of 18 magazines around the world, walked the red carpet at Cannes, been named an influencer, and won TEDx awards. “I’ve always been a business owner in everything I’ve done. “It’s the Punjabi in me,” says Büller-Khosla with a smile.
