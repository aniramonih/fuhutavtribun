Harold Ford Jr Educational Background

He graduated from the exclusive St. Albans School in Washington, D.C., a prominent university preparatory school.

He attended Double Tree Elementary School, a public Montessori school in the Westwood district after his father was elected to Congress.

Later, in 1992, he graduated with a B.A. in American history from the University of Pennsylvania.

Ford went back to school and graduated from the University of Michigan Law School in 1996 with a J.D.

Career Beginnings

How did Harold Ford Jr’s career begin?

In 1996, he began his political career when he was chosen to represent Tennessee’s 9th congressional district in the U.S. House of Representatives.

Since Reconstruction, he was the first African American elected to Congress from Tennessee.

He served on the House Committee on Budget, the House Committee on Education and the Workforce, and the House Financial Services Committee during his term in Congress.

Ford Jr. lost against Republican Bob Corker in his quest for the U.S. Senate from Tennessee in 2006.

He became a vice chairman and senior policy advisor at the investment bank Merrill Lynch after losing. He was appointed managing director at Morgan Stanley in 2011.

In addition to his work in politics and business, Harold Ford Jr frequently contributes as a pundit to news channels like MSNBC, CNBC, and Fox News. Additionally, he has written opinion pieces for a number of newspapers, such as The New York Times, The Wall Street Journal, and The Huffington Post.
