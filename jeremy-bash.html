Jeremy Bash

August 2024 · 3 minute read

Jeremy Bash is an American lawyer who works for Beacon Global Strategies LLC as a managing director. He also works for NBC News and MSNBC as a national security analyst. He was formerly the chief of staff of the United States Department of Defense and the Central Intelligence Agency.

Jeremy Bash was born on August 13, 1971, in Arlington, Virginia, under the zodiac sign of Leo. The 49-year-old lawyer is of Jewish ancestry and is of American nationality. In 1989, he received his diploma from the Charles E. Smith Jewish Day School. While at Georgetown University, he was the editor-in-chief of the student newspaper The Hoya. He also graduated with honors from Harvard Law School in 1998 with a J.D.

Bash began clerking for Leonie Brinkema, a United States District Judge, after graduation. He was later admitted to the Eastern District of Virginia’s bar and the Fourth Circuit Court of Appeals’ bar.

Bash started working as the national security issues chief for Joe Lieberman and Al Gore’s presidential campaigns in 2000. From 2001 to 2004, he worked as an attorney with the Washington, D.C.-based firm O’Melveny & Myers. He then worked as an aide to California Representative Jane Harman and as chief minority counsel on the US House of Representatives’ Permanent Select Committee on Intelligence.

Bash was also a member of the Council on Foreign Relations’ team in the past. Under President Barack Obama, he even served as the chief of staff of the Central Intelligence Agency from 2009 to 2011 and the US Department of Defense from 2011 to 2013.

Bash is currently a managing director at Beacon Global Strategies LLC, which he co-founded with Andrew Shapiro and Philippe Reines in 2013. He also works for NBC News and MSNBC as a national security analyst.

Bash’s net worth is expected to be around $45 million by 2020.

Wife, Children, and Married:

Bash has had two marriages. On September 6, 1998, he married his first wife, Dana Ruth Schwartz. Dana, his now ex-wife, worked as an assignment editor in the Cable News Network’s Washington bureau at the time. The couple was unable to maintain their marriage and divorced in 2007.

Bash married Robyn Cooke, whom he met in 2007, two years later. Cooke and he have three daughters together. For personal purposes, he has chosen not to reveal the identities of his children.

Siblings, Parents, and Family:

The American lawyer comes from a family of Conservative Jews. Marvin Bash and Deborah Blumenthal Bash were his parents. Marvin, his father, was the chief rabbi of the Arlington Fairfax Jewish Congregation. Meanwhile, his mother is the former program director of Georgetown University’s Graduate Education Nurse-Midwifery Program.

Bash was raised by his three brothers. Brother Rachmiel and sister Gila are his older brothers. He has a twin sister as well.

Height and Weight:

Bash stands at a respectable 5 feet 8 inches tall. His hair is dark brown, and his eyes are brown.
