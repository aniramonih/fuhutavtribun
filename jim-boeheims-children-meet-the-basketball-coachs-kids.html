Meet the Basketball Coachs Kids

August 2024 · 15 minute read

Jim Boeheim’s Children are four in number and they include Buddy Boeheim, Jamie Boeheim, Jimmy Boeheim, and Elizabeth Boeheim.

The college basketball coach is a doting father who has bonded really well with his children over the years. Their family is close-knit and spends a lot of time with each other. Some of his children have followed in his footsteps to become basketball players themselves. Find out more about them here.

Jim Boeheim’s Children Are From His Two Marriages

Jim Boeheim, whose full name is James Arthur Boeheim Jr, has been married two times so far. His first marriage was to a woman called Elaine Boeheim. According to our findings, the former lovebirds first met in the early 1970s and started dating. They eventually got married in 1976 and set their home in Syracuse, New York.

At this time, Jim was still an up-and-coming figure in the basketball coaching ranks and was serving as an assistant coach with the Syracuse basketball team. While they were married, Jim and Elaine shared a daughter together called Elizabeth Boeheim. The couple’s marriage lasted for about 17 years before they parted ways and divorced in 1994. The reason for the divorce is not very clear but some say it had to do with Jim’s blossoming career which impacted the family.

Following his divorce from Elaine, Jim Boeheim went on to meet his second wife, a woman called Juli Boeheim. The two are said to have met in 1994 at a Kentucky Derby party in Lexington. They had attended the event individually but were later introduced to each other by a mutual friend. They started dating and soon decided to tie the knot. They then got married on October 10, 1997.

Jim and Juli Boeheim have remained married since they tied the knot in 1997 and their romance appears to be getting stronger. They share a deep passion for philanthropy and are now parents to three children whom they had together. Altogether, Jim Boeheim now has four children; two daughters and two sons.

She Suffered A Booing Incident

Jamie suffered what can be described as a bullying incident in 2015 while she was playing for Jamesville-DeWitt High School. At the time, she and her teammates were facing off against Albertus Magnus High School in the New York State playoffs when several people on the opposing end began to jeer at her.

Some of the fans chanted “Where’s the cheater?” at her and tried to distract her from the game. However, she and her team later went on to cruise to victory. At the end of the game, the principal of Albertus Magnus High School, Joseph T. Troy sent a formal apology letter to Jamie and her teammates for what had happened.

Jamie also did well with blocks, ranking 11th in the UAA in total blocks.
