Juju Smith-Schuster Parents And Siblings

JuJu Smith-Schuster Family Roots can be traced back to Samoan and African-American ancestry, and he was raised in Long Beach, California.

Identifying as a Christian, he is the second child in a family of seven siblings. He is also related to Johnny Nansen, who previously served as an assistant coach at USC.

He earned his nickname “JuJu” when he was just a few months old, given to him by his aunt. She initially addressed him as “John-John,” but later switched to “JuJu.”

So’omalo is his only biological sister, and their Father was not involved in their upbringing.

When he was four years old, his mother, Sammy (Toa), was introduced to Lawrence Schuster, who later became his stepfather.

Lawrence is truly a Father figure for him as he hyphenated Schuster to his last name after he turned 18 as a way of honouring his stepfather.

Schuster looked up to USC as a child and found role models in wide receivers such as Marqise Lee, Robert Woods, and Nelson Agholor.

The wide receiver’s mother, Sammy Schuster, had five additional children with Lawrence after JuJu’s birth, and he often takes care of them when he visits his hometown in South Carolina.
