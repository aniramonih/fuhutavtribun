Leeroy Thornhill Overview and Wiki

Early Life and Entry into Music

Born in Barking, Essex, but raised in Rayne near Braintree, Leeroy Thornhill‘s life in music began after he met Liam Howlett at a local rave. This encounter led him to join The Prodigy, alongside Keith Flint and Maxim Reality.

Standing at an impressive 6 feet 7 inches, Thornhill was a striking figure, his height towering over Flint by a full foot. His early interests in football and James Brown shaped his rhythmic sense, which he brought to the group’s energetic live performances.

Evolving Role and Performances

Throughout his time with The Prodigy, Thornhill was not only a dancer but also contributed as a keyboardist on occasion, demonstrating his versatility within the band.

His dynamic presence was integral during the band’s global tours and numerous music video appearances, including hits like Firestarter and Breathe. These tracks not only dominated the charts but also helped to define the visual and performance style of the rave and electronic music scene of that era.

Solo Career and Independent Projects

After departing from The Prodigy in 2000, Thornhill ventured into solo projects under the pseudonyms Longman and Flightcrank. This period of his career allowed him to explore a range of musical styles, producing various solo EPs that showcased his ability to transcend beyond the rave scene into more nuanced electronic compositions.

His talent for remixing was highlighted through his work on tracks for other artists, like the remix for Italian band Subsonica’s Nuvole Rapide.

Continued Influence as a DJ

Beyond his solo projects, Thornhill continued to make waves in the music industry as a DJ, performing at large venues and festivals, including a notable appearance at the V Festival in 2007.

His deep connection with The Prodigy was maintained as he served as the DJ support act on their Their Law tour, affirming his enduring ties to the band and its members.

Entrepreneurial Ventures

Most recently, Thornhill has embraced the role of a music entrepreneur with the launch of his record label, Electric Tastebuds. This label focuses on the nu skool breaks genre, signing emerging talents like The Wrongstar Society and further influencing the electronic music scene.

His label demonstrates his commitment to nurturing new artists and introducing fresh sounds to the electronic music landscape.

Innovative Collaborations and Current Projects

In 2008, Thornhill was involved in the Smash Hi-Fi project, which led to the creation of a music video for the track Everything U Need, directed by Philip Carrer and Bleeding Apple. This project is just one example of his ongoing innovation in music production and visual storytelling.
