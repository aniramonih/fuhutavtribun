Biography of Nick Rhodes

Nick Rhodes, born Nicholas James Bates on June 8, 1962, has carved a prominent place for himself in the annals of music history as an English keyboardist and producer. His most enduring claim to fame is his role as a founding member and the keyboardist of the iconic band Duran Duran. Affectionately known among his peers and fans as “the Controller,” this moniker was playfully introduced by his bandmate Simon Le Bon during the Astronaut album world tours of 2004–2005, underscoring his significance within the band.

Rhodes’ journey into the world of music began against a backdrop of prosperity and comfort. He was the only child of affluent parents who owned a well-regarded toy shop in Birmingham. His academic journey led him to Woodrush High School in Wythall, north Worcestershire. However, his path diverged from the conventional trajectory as he made a bold decision to leave school in 1978 at the young age of sixteen.

It was during this pivotal period that he, alongside his childhood friend John Taylor and Taylor’s art school acquaintance Stephen Duffy, founded Duran Duran. They experimented with various band names before settling on the iconic title, “Duran Duran,” inspired by Milo O’Shea’s character, Dr. Durand Durand, in the sci-fi film Barbarella.

The early days of Duran Duran were marked by the recording of their first songs on a simple cassette tape recorder above Bates’ parents’ toy shop. Their first gig occurred on April 5, 1979, at Birmingham Polytechnic, with the subsequent addition of Simon Colley, who contributed on clarinet and bass. Following a series of changes in personnel, the band underwent significant transformations and eventually solidified its lineup in May 1980, which included guitarist Andy Taylor and lead singer Simon Le Bon. This marked the moment of their signing to EMI.

Nick Rhodes is a name that evolved from his original name, Nicholas Bates. The change was made for aesthetic reasons and was solidified during Duran Duran’s first official interview. Rhodes’ contribution to Duran Duran was instrumental, especially in shaping their distinctive sound. An unschooled musician, he delved into experimenting with the sounds of analogue synthesizers.

His unique contributions include the distinct warble in “Save a Prayer,” the keyboard stabs in “A View to a Kill,” and the memorable string sounds in songs like “Come Undone” and “Ordinary World.” Furthermore, he played a significant role in popularizing the Crumar Performer on the early records. Nick Rhodes was not only a master of sound but also a visionary when it came to music videos, urging the band to invest more effort into their videos long before MTV became a major platform.

Rhodes’ involvement extended beyond the keyboard, as he began contributing lyrics and music to Duran Duran in the late 1990s. His digitally altered voice can be heard on the title track of the 1997 album “Medazzaland.” He also dabbled in record production, showcasing his skills while working in the studio with the band. This even extended to co-producing several tracks on their acclaimed “Rio” album. Moreover, he co-produced the debut single “Too Shy” for the band Kajagoogoo, which became a UK No. 1 hit in 1983.

Outside of Duran Duran, Nick Rhodes ventured into side projects that expanded his creative horizons. He formed the band Arcadia with Simon Le Bon, departing from Duran Duran’s signature sound to explore a moody, keyboard-heavy atmosphere. Their hit single “Election Day” and album “So Red the Rose” achieved notable success in the United States, if not in their native UK.

Additionally, Rhodes reunited with the original Duran Duran vocalist, Stephen Duffy, to create music based on their earliest compositions. This collaboration resulted in the album “Dark Circles,” released under the moniker “The Devils.” Rhodes also worked on a side project called TV Mania with former Duran Duran guitarist Warren Cuccurullo, releasing the album “Bored with Prozac and the Internet?” in 2013.

Beyond his musical pursuits, Nick Rhodes is an individual of varied interests and talents. An art student in his formative years, he cultivated connections in the art world and developed friendships with luminaries like Andy Warhol. He has exhibited his photography at various venues, with one of his exhibitions titled “BEI INCUBI (Beautiful Nightmares)” taking place in Chelsea, London, in 2013.

In recognition of his significant contributions to the music industry, Rhodes was awarded an Honorary Doctorate of Arts degree by the University of Bedfordshire in 2011. His culinary preferences reveal his penchant for Italian, Lebanese, and Indian cuisine, and his vegetarian lifestyle attests to his conscious choices. He continues to reside in central London, a city that has been his home for many years.

Nick Rhodes’ career is intertwined with the history of Duran Duran, and his creative energy has extended to various side projects and artistic endeavors. His legacy as a pioneering musician and a visionary artist continues to be celebrated by fans and peers alike. With a career marked by experimentation and innovation, Nick Rhodes remains a driving force in the world of music and art.
