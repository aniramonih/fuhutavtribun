The Tragic Loss of Pava LaPere: A Rising Star in the Tech World

Pava Marie LaPere was a 26-year-old entrepreneur and the founder and CEO of EcoMap Technologies[2]. Born on January 21, 1997, in Tucson, Arizona, she was the daughter of Caroline and Frank LaPere[6]. Pava attended Johns Hopkins University, where she earned a Bachelor of Arts in Sociology[6]. During her time at university, she co-founded and served as the President of TCO Labs, a nonprofit organization dedicated to strengthening the entrepreneurial ecosystem at Johns Hopkins University[6].

LaPere founded EcoMap Technologies at the age of 22 in her dorm room[2]. The company focused on hyperlocal, ecosystem-based economic development to create a more equitable future for all communities[2]. Her workforce was 50% female and 50% people of color, and her clients included The Aspen Institute, Meta, the WXR Fund, and T.Rowe Price Foundation[2]. In 2023, she was listed in Forbes' "30 Under 30" class for social impact and was recognized in Baltimore's 40 Under 40 and Maryland's 25 Under 25 lists for innovation[2].
