Early Life

Robert Selden Duvall hails from San Diego, California, where he was born on January 5, 1931. His mother, Mildred Virginia, was an amateur actress, and his father, William Howard Duvall, was U.S Navy admiral. When he was young, he spent most of his time in Annapolis, Maryland, which was the site of U.S Naval Academy.

Duvall did his schooling at Severn School in Maryland and completed his Bachelor of Arts degree in Drama from Principia College in Elsah, Illinois, in the year 1951.

Robert Duvall served in the United States Army from August 1953 to August 1954 after the Korean War.

He was in New York in 1955, where he studied at the Neighborhood Playhouse School of the Theatre and also attended Meisner’s classes. While studying, he met Gene Hackman, James Caan, and Dustin Hoffman, where they rented an apartment together. The three roommates have come a long way since and earned collectively 19 Academy Award nominations along with five wins.

Career

Duvall’s television debut was for Armstrong Circle Theatre in the year 1959, but his career escalated when he acted on the movie To Kill A Mockingbird.

Duvall’s biggest breakthrough came when he played the character of Tom Hagen in the 1972 movie The Godfather. He played some notable supporting roles in the movies The Eagle Has Landed and Apocalypse Now, which earned him a Golden Globe award.
