Salma Paralluelo Family Background & Ethnicity

Paralluelo comes from a diverse family background. She was born to a Spanish father and an Equatorial Guinean Fang mother on 13 November 2003.

The winger’s father is Jaime Paralluelo and her mother is Diosdada Ayingono. Jamie held numerous jobs as an electrician, and truck driver to raise the family. Her mother Diosdada went to Switzerland to work because of the economic crisis in the family.

The middle of three children, she has two brothers named José Jaime and Lorenzo Paralluelo. Her siblings are also pursuing a career in football.

Her Career Journey

Paralluelo played youth-level football for her hometown club UD San José and Zaragoza CFF. She played reserve teams for Zaragoza CFF in the lower divisions.

In 2019, she joined the Liga F team Villarreal and played for three seasons. She joined Barcelona in 2022 and now plays as a left-winger for the team.


Salma has played for the Spain youth team and has won the 2018 UEFA Women’s Under-17 Championship, the 2018 FIFA U-17 Women’s World Cup, and the 2022 FIFA U-20 Women’s World Cup. She made her senior debut in a friendly against Argentina on 11 November 2022.

Prior to that, she was a sprinter and participated in numerous events. She won two gold medals in the 400m hurdles event in the 2019 European Youth Summer Olympic Festival.
