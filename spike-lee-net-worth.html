What is Spike Lee’s Net Worth and Salary?

Spike Lee is a renowned American movie director, writer, producer, actor, and college professor with a net worth of $60 million. He co-founded the production company 40 Acres and a Mule, and has produced over 35 films since 1983.

Lee made his directorial debut with “She’s Gotta Have It” in 1986 and has since directed many critically acclaimed and commercially successful films, including “Do the Right Thing,” “Jungle Fever,” and “Malcolm X.”

He earned his graduate degree in filmmaking from NYU’s Tisch School of the Arts and is the first student director to have a film featured in Lincoln Center’s “New Directors, New Films” Festival.

Lee’s films often touch on controversial topics such as race and gun violence, and he has been nominated for two Academy Awards. However, he has also been involved in public feuds with politicians and fellow filmmakers.

Spike Lee: Early Life and Education

Spike Lee was born Shelton Jackson Lee in Atlanta, Georgia on March 20, 1957. He attended Brooklyn’s John Dewey High School and later enrolled at Morehouse College in Atlanta, where he graduated with a B.A. in mass communications. Lee went on to earn his Master of Fine Arts (MFA) degree in film and television from New York University’s Tisch School of the Arts in 1978. His first student film, “Last Hustle in Brooklyn,” was made while he was at Morehouse College. Lee’s independent film, “Joe’s Bed-Stuy Barbershop: We Cut Heads,” was the first student film to be showcased in Lincoln Center’s New Directors/New Films Festival.

Lee’s parents were Jacqueline, who taught arts and black literature, and William, a jazz musician and composer. He has three younger siblings, Joie, David, and Cinque. His mother gave him the nickname Spike when he was a young boy. The family moved from Atlanta to Brooklyn when he was a child.

Spike Lee’s Career and Accomplishments

Spike Lee began his film career with his first feature film, She’s Gotta Have It, in 1985. Despite having a budget of only $175,000, the film grossed over $7 million in the U.S. box office. Lee’s films often explore race relations, colorism in the black community, and political issues. His production company, 40 Acres & A Mule, has produced over 35 films since 1983. Lee’s films are commonly referred to as “Spike Lee Joints,” and he often appears in cameo roles.

Lee received an Academy Honorary Award in 2015 for his contributions to film. His 2018 film, BlacKkKlansman, won the Grand Prix at the 2018 Cannes Film Festival and was nominated for the Academy Award for Best Picture and Best Director, which was Lee’s first-ever nomination in this category. He won his first competitive Academy Award in the category Best Adapted Screenplay for the same film. Lee’s reoccurring motifs include a baseball-related theme and a character that appears to be gliding instead of walking.

Spike Lee’s Teaching Career and Commercial Directing

In 1991, Lee taught a course on filmmaking at Harvard. He then went on to teach at New York University’s Tisch School of the Arts graduate film program starting from 1993 and is now a tenured professor at NYU.

Along with his film career, Lee has also directed commercials for various companies through the marketing division of 40 Acres and a Mule. Some of the companies he has worked with include Converse, Jaguar, Taco Bell, and Ben & Jerry’s.

Spike Lee’s Love for New York Sports

Spike Lee, who married attorney Tonya Lewis in 1993, is a devoted fan of New York sports teams, including the Yankees, Knicks, Rangers, and Arsenal. He has been a regular courtside presence at Knicks games for nearly 30 years, estimated to have spent $10 million on tickets over his lifetime.

Spike Lee’s Earnings as a Movie Director

Spike Lee earned $3 million for directing the 1992 film “Malcolm X.” It is assumed that he earned at least $3 million in salary, and likely more in the backend for other movies he directed, such as “25th Hour,” “Love & Basketball,” “Summer of Sam,” and “Inside Man.”

Spike Lee’s Properties

Spike Lee’s main residence since 1998 has been a 9,000-square-foot townhome located in Manhattan’s most expensive Upper East Side zip code. The mansion is actually made up of two three-story buildings with a shared central courtyard. Spike listed the home for sale in 2014 for $32 million. He did not find a buyer and continues to own the property to this day.

Spike’s film production company Forty Acres and a Mule is located in a three-story building in Brooklyn that he purchased in 1991 for $820,000. Comparable buildings on the same block are currently selling for $2-$4 million.

When Spike isn’t kicking back in New York City, you might find him at his two-acre estate in Martha’s Vineyard. Located just off the 18th hole of Farm Neck Golf Club, Spike purchased the property in 1989 for $400,000. He then built a four-bedroom mansion which today is worth an estimated $3-4 million.
