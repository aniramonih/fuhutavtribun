Early Life

Steven Elliot Tisch was born on the 14th of February, 1949 in Lakewood Township, New Jersey to Preston Robert (Bob Tisch) and Joan Tisch. His family is of Jewish background. Steve’s father was also a TV and film executive as well as the co-owner of the ‘New York Giants.’

Even though Steve had the family business as an easy option, he was intrigued by the cinematography and decided to take it up as a profession. When he grew up, he began shaping his career in the filmmaking industry. The first steps he took towards his dream were after he joined Tufts University. With the help of ‘Columbia Pictures,’ he created quite a few small films.

Personal Life

Steve Tisch married twice. The first marriage ended after the couple got divorced. After that, Steve was married to Jamie Leigh Anne Alexander with whom he had a boy and two girls. He currently resides in Los Angeles, New York.

Career

Steve Tisch’s career took a turn in 1976 when he made his debut feature film, ‘Outlaw Blues’ after he left Colombia. In 1983, he created ‘Risky Business’ with Tom Cruise in the leading role. It was the first time that Tom Cruise depicted the main character. In the year 1984, Steve produced a direct on TV movie named ‘The Burning Bed,’ which was quite controversial.

Even then, due to the actress Farrah Fawcett’s incredible role of a distressed and assaulted wife, the film ended up receiving a total of eleven Emmy Awards nominations. After his growing success as a film producer, Steve decided to start up a production company of his own. He opened a production house named ‘Steve Tisch Company’ in the year 1986.

The company got recognized for its specialization in small screen movies. Though, there are some big-screen hits delivered by Steve’s company too, such as ‘Snatch,’ ‘Forrest Gump’ and ‘American History X.’ His film, ‘Forrest Gump’ was widely praised by the critics and ended up being nominated for a total of thirteen Academy Awards out of which it won six.

He merged his company with Todd Black and Jason Blumenthal’s named ‘Black and Blu’ to form an independent company at Sony Pictures Entertainment called ‘Escape Artists.’ The joint venture has produced many movies since its establishment including ‘The Wheather Man’ (2005), ‘The Pursuit of Happyness’ (2006), Seven Pounds (2008), Knowing (2009), and ‘The Taking of Pelham 123’ (2009). He became the EVP and chairman of the Giants in 2005. Due to his achievements and business knack, he was even invited in season 5 of the reality show, ‘Shark Tank.’
