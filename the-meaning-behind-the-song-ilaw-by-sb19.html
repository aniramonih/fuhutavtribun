The Meaning Behind The Song: ILAW by SB19

August 2024 · 4 minute read

Introduction

As a fan of SB19, one of the most popular Filipino boy bands, I have always been captivated by the deep emotions and meaningful lyrics behind their songs. One track that has resonated with me on a personal level is “ILAW,” which is part of their album titled PAGTATAG! released in 2023. In this article, I will delve into the meaning of this powerful song and share some of my own experiences with it.

Exploring the Lyrics

“ILAW” starts with verses sung by Ken and Josh, portraying a moment of self-reflection where they find themselves smiling, yet masking their true emotions. The lyrics express their struggle to reveal their inner selves, emphasizing the repetitiveness of concealing their true identity. The line “Saang lupalop ko ba ‘to naitabi?” suggests a feeling of being lost, as if they have misplaced a part of who they are.

The pre-chorus, sung by Justin, represents the confusion and disconnection they feel from the world around them. They question where their true identity has gone, as if it has been lost or overshadowed by external influences.

The chorus, featuring Pablo and Stell, is a powerful plea to be heard. They ask, “Sino ba’ng may tenga sa mga bulong ko?” which translates to “Who will listen to my whispers?” They express their frustration at being misunderstood and unheard, even when they shout. The lyrics plead for someone to open the door to the future, so they can finally be seen for who they truly are, hiding behind the blinding light.

In the second verse, Justin, Stell, Ken, and Pablo continue to explore their struggle with self-expression. They mention the temporary nature of the light they seek, as they acknowledge their need to rest and momentarily extinguish that light.

The pre-chorus sung by Josh reflects on their hope for change, questioning whether their transformation will lead to their disappearance. There is a sense of fear and uncertainty about losing oneself in the process of changing.

The bridge, performed by Josh and Justin, depicts a moment of contemplation as they head towards their own “presinto,” which represents a metaphorical jail, struggling with their own thoughts and anxieties. They express a desire for a break, even if it is just for a week, as they question the purpose of their existence.

The post-bridge, sung by Ken and Stell, portrays the weight they carry with every step they take, heading towards a place they do not want to go. They reflect on their ongoing search for hope, but question the significance of their very breath.

The breakdown sung by Pablo is an intense repetition of “Sino?” which emphasizes their unanswered plea for someone who will listen and understand them.

The final chorus, performed by Pablo and Stell, repeats the earlier plea to be heard. They express their frustration with being misunderstood and emphasize their desire to reveal their true selves, hidden behind the blinding light.

Personal Connection

Personally, “ILAW” resonates with me on a deep level. As someone who has experienced moments of self-doubt and struggled to find their true identity, the lyrics speak to the emotions I have felt. The song acts as a reminder that there are others who feel the same way and experience the same challenges in expressing themselves authentically.

SB19’s ability to convey these emotions through their music is truly inspiring. Their lyrics and vocal performances make it easier for fans like me to connect with their music on an emotional level. “ILAW” is a reminder that it’s important to find the courage to be true to ourselves, even if it means facing the challenges that come with it.

Conclusion

“ILAW” by SB19 is a powerful song that explores the struggle of self-expression and the longing to be genuinely understood. With its deeply relatable lyrics and emotional vocal performances, the song captures the essence of the band’s message. It reminds us to embrace our true identities and find the strength to break free from the barriers that hold us back. SB19 continues to touch the hearts of their fans through their music, and “ILAW” is undoubtedly an anthem for those who have ever felt invisible or unheard.
