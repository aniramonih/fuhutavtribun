Kaine was raised in Overland Park, Kansas, and was born in Saint Paul, Minnesota. He attended the University of Missouri in Columbia, Missouri, and the Harvard Law School, where he received his Juris Doctor degree, before starting his own practice and working as a lecturer at the University of Richmond School of Law.

His first election to public service was for a Richmond City Council seat in 1994. He was chosen as Richmond’s mayor in 1998 and served in that capacity until he was chosen as Virginia’s lieutenant governor in 2001.

Kaine was chosen as Virginia’s governor in 2005, serving in that position from 2006 until 2010. From 2009 to 2011, he served as the Democratic National Committee’s chairman.

Kaine graduated from the University of Missouri with a Bachelor of Arts in economics in 1979, finishing in three years with honors from Omicron Delta Kappa and summa cum laude.

In 1978, he was a fellow at the Coro Foundation in Kansas City. After starting at Harvard Law School in 1979, he took a year off to work in Honduras for nine months in 1980 and 1981, assisting Jesuit missionaries who were running a Catholic school in El Progreso.

He managed a vocational school that taught welding and carpentry, and he also assisted in boosting enrollment by enlisting neighborhood residents. Because of his time spent in Honduras, Kaine speaks Spanish fluently.
