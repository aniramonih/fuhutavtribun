Tim Norman Faces Federal Charges Of Conspiring In Murder Of His Nephew Andre Montgomery

August 2024 · 3 minute read

Welcome to Sweetie Pie's star Tim Norman, whose real name is James Timothy Norman is dealing with federal charges for allegedly conspiring with an exotic dancer, Teria Ellis, in the murder of his nephew Andre Montgomery. As per the US attorney, the authority arrested Tim Norman from his home in Jackson, Mississippi, on 18 August 2020.

The federal authorities have cited that Norman played a severe conspiracy to have his eighteen years old nephew murdered back in 2016. A criminal complaint that got unsealed on 18 August 2020 suggests Norman took $450,000 in life insurance policies on his nephew Andre Montgomery in 2014.

Mugshot of Tim Norman (©: riverfronttimes.com)

As of now, he is held at a jail in Madison County, Mississippi, without any lieu. Both Norman and Ellis got charged with conspiracy to use interstate commerce facilities in 'commission of murder-for-hire,' which resulted in death.

The St. Louis police and the FBI are investigating the murder case that happened four years back in 2016.

The 2016's Murder Case of Andre Montgomery

The phone records obtained by the investigators show that Norman and the Memphis-based dancer named Teria Ellis had communication over the burner phones. The documents he further indicates that Ellis started contacting Montgomery and sent him her Instagram account handle, Alexusdagreat. She also texted him that she would be visiting him in St. Louis.

The email Ellis sent to Montgomery on 10 March 2016 reads, 'I'm on my way in town.'

The 41-year-old reality star (Norman) flew to St. Louis three days after the email was sent to Montgomery. He booked a room at the Chase Park Plaza hotel, and Ellis asked him to find where Montgomery was on the following day, on 14 March 2016. As the records obtained by the cell-phone investigations, Norman texted the house address; 3964 Natural Bridge Ave to Ellis.

At around 8:45 p.m., Andre Montgomery was shot to death at his house. Investigators believe that Ellis was there at the scene as she made a call to Norman just a minute after Montgomery got shot. Norman then flew back to Los Angeles early in the morning after the murder.

On the other hand, Ellis began depositing money, around $3,020, in a checking account, which had a negative balance. She also opened another savings account on the same day and kept $4,340. Similarly, Ellis credited another $1900 in the same account. She flew back to Los Angeles with her mother and daughter on 22 March 2016.

In the case of Tim Norman, he tried collecting Andre Montgomery's life insurance policy for around a week. He even called a bank on 21 March 2016 without the required documentation.

Net Worth Of Tim Norman

Tim Norman, as a reality star, musician, and entrepreneur, has summoned a net worth of $500 thousand. He previously served in prison for ten years for armed robbery. However, after the release, Norman started working with his family business, The Original Sweetie Pies. Similarly, he took over the management of Sweetie Pies at the Mangrove.

Tim Norman and 'Welcome to Sweet Pies' casts (©: Access)

As a reality star, he starred in Oprah Winfrey Network reality series, 'Welcome to Sweetie Pies.' Besides that, he has some history of social work. He was a member of the Boys & Girls Club, from where he works to inspire self-confidence and guide the youths to the positive path.
