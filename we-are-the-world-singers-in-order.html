We Are the World Singers In Order

August 2024 · 7 minute read

We Are the World in order include 1. Lionel Richie 2. Stevie Wonder 3. Paul Simon, and more. The ensemble was conducted by Quincy Jones.

The song was released by the Supergroup USA for Africa as a benefit single for the African famine relief. It was the brainchild of musician and activist Harry Belafonte who had been inspired by the UK group Band-Aid’s song, Do They Know It’s Christmas?

Written by Michael Jackson and Lionel Ritchie and produced by Michael Omartian and Quincy Jones, the track was recorded on January 28, 1985, and featured 21 solo singers and over 23 singers on the chorus. It proved a huge success and has since gone on to become the 8th best-selling physical single of all time.

We Are the World Singers Names With Pictures

We Are the World Singers Names With Pictures include some of the biggest names of that era. Plus, these artists joined together from different genres.

As stated above, the group was split into two categories when it came to singers. There were the soloists, who each got some lines by themselves, and the chorus, who joined in during the chorus.

The soloists are as follows:

Lionel Richie

Lionel Richie is one of the most popular singers of his era. Now, he is best known for being one of the judges on American Idol.

Stevie Wonder

Stevie Wonder is one of the best male singers of the 70s and 80s. He made history by winning two consecutive Grammy awards by the age of 25.

Paul Simon

Paul Simon is best known for both being one half of the folk duo Simon & Garfunkel and for his solo career. He is one of the pioneers of World music.

Kenny Rogers

Kenny Rogers is one of the richest Country music singers of all time. Rogers is known for his powerful voice and amazing songwriting.

James Ingram

James Ingram was a two-time Grammy Award-winning singer and songwriter. Sadly, he passed away on January 29, 2019.

Tina Turner

Tina Turner is widely known as the Queen of Rock and Roll. She is regarded as one of the best female vocalists of all time.

Billy Joel

Billy Joel rivals Elton John for the title of THE piano man. This crooner has been making music since the 60s.

Michael Jackson

Michael Jackon will forever be remembered as the King of Pop. He was one of the chief architects behind the We Are the World project.

Diana Ross

Diana Ross is one of the best female soul singers of the 60s. Her powerful voice laid the template for many singers, male or female, who followed.

Dionne Warwick

Dionne Warwick is not only a singer and actress but an institution. She has sold more than 100 million records globally.

Willie Nelson

Willie Nelson is an iconic Country musician best known for his immense catalog. The Willie Nelson Hollywood Bowl 2023 will celebrate his honor.

Al Jarreau

Al Jarreau was a popular Grammy Award-winning singer. His career saw its zenith reached in the 1980s.

Bruce Springsteen

Bruce Springsteen is an amazing singer and songwriter. Known lovingly as “The Boss” Springsteen continues to churn out great music.

Kenny Loggins

Kenny Loggins is a popular Country and Rock singer. He is best known for his song Footloose.

Steve Perry

Steve Perry is best known as the most prominent lead singer of the band Journey. He left the band in the 80s and has since had a successful solo career.

Daryl Hall

Daryl Hall is best known as one half of the Soul duo Hall & Oates. Hall is best remembered for his many crossover singles.

Huey Lewis

Huey Lewis is best known for fronting the Pop group Huey Lewis and the News. He is also popularly referenced in the movie, American Psycho.

Cyndi Lauper

Cyndi Lauper is best known for her breakout hit Girls Just Wanna Have Fun. The singer is also known for her outlandish style and hair.

Kim Carnes

Kim Carnes is a popular singer who rose in popularity in the 70s. Prior to that, she had worked as a session singer in the 60s.

Bob Dylan

Bob Dylan is one of the most important singer-songwriters in the history of Rock. He is the only Rockstar with a Nobel Prize.

Ray Charles

Ray Charles was an iconic singer known for his immense talent despite his blindness. From singing to the piano to the saxophone, he could do it all.

We Are The World 1985 Lyrics And Singers

We Are The World 1985 Lyrics And Singers feature 21 singers sharing the verses of the song. In the chorus, they are joined by a further 23 people.

The song begins with Lionel Richie singing two lines of the first verse with Paul Simon completing it with the next three lines. Richie then sings the second verse by himself.

Michael Jackson and Diana Ross then sing the chorus, with Jackson going first for three lines, Ross with the next two, and them together with the last two. Ritchie once again sings the third verse by himself, as well as the following chorus.

The Bridge then is sung my Jackson, Huey Lewis, Cyndi Lauper, and Kim Carnes, who each take a line. The entire choir joins them for the next chorus, and as they go into the next chorus, Bob Dylan comes in to deliver four lines.

The choir keeps singing the chorus with Ray Charles then coming to deliver some lines alongside them. Stevie Wonder and Bruce Springsteen sing the chorus together twice, and then the choir continues singing the chorus a couple times.
